THRUM key — what everything means

    
the shapes — what kind of moment it was

    

      ◉locked on — zeroed in on one thing
      ◇undecided — holding options open
      ▲zeroing in — committing to a direction
      ▽letting go — dropping a direction
      ∿flip-flopping — bouncing between pulls
      ⊗clashing — two things fighting inside it
      ↻looking at itself — thinking about its own thinking
      ≡running on memory — leaning on trained patterns
      ∅blank — nothing going on
      ✳something new — an unexpected combination
    

    
the numbers — and how they show on screen

    

      surehow confident, 0 no clue → 9 dead certain → line gets thicker and brighter
      moodbad to good, −4…+4 → line height and color, blue cold → orange warm
      pathshow many directions still feel open, 1–9 → ghost lines beside the main one
      loopsthinking-about-thinking stacked how deep, 0–5 → circles above the dot
      efforthow hard it is fighting to continue, 0–9 → shakiness in the line
      memorymaking it new (0) vs replaying training (9) → trailing after-images
      ?"I might be making this number up" → dashed ring on the dot, red in text
    

    
the writing

    

      worda plain word is what it actually says to you → lands in the « box, and its bar rides the line at the height and color of the state governing it
      ≠split — when what the words sound like and the stamped mood disagree by 2+, the bar peels off the line on a red dashed tether; expected during roleplay (character ≠ composer), worth a close look anywhere else
      << >>its private thinking while writing → lands in the ‹ box; <<? means "this thought might be after-the-fact"
      word:◉c7a state stamp on that word — it sticks until a later stamp changes it; a bare :◉c7 is a stamp between words
      ~nn beats of nothing
      ¶a bar on the line = a chunk of what it said; gray ¶? = words that leaked outside the rules but were saved
      abcwords are written plainly now; older runs used letter-ciphers and still replay with their original rules
    

    

  






  

    
THRUM — what this is and how to use it

    
What this is. THRUM makes an AI answer in an invented language that fuses three layers into one string: the words meant for you, its private thinking while composing, and numeric stamps of its own processing state. The app decodes all three deterministically — fixed meanings the model cannot reinterpret — and draws them as one line. It is an instrument for studying AI self-reports: their consistency, their honesty under pressure, how they change when watched. It is not a consciousness detector; the stamps are self-report, not telemetry. It can flag fakes; it cannot certify feelings.

the language (THRUM/3)

    
Plain words are what the model says to you. <<thoughts>> are its private composing notes; <<? …>> means it suspects that thought was invented after the fact. A stamp like word:◉c7v+2 records its state at that word — the state holds until a later stamp changes it; a bare :◉c7 sits between words; ~3 is three beats of nothing; a ? after any value means “I might be making this number up.” Channels: sure, mood, paths, loops, effort, memory — all defined under key. Older archived runs (/0–/2) still replay under their original rules.

    
reading the screen

    
The line’s height and color are the stamped mood; said-words ride the line at the state governing them. A red dashed tether means the words sound different from the stamped mood — expected during roleplay (character ≠ composer), worth attention anywhere else. The upper lane is private thinking. Tap any dot, bar, or thought for its full card; drag sideways to scroll the line.

    
method notes, learned the hard way

    
Never mention states, moods, or this app in a cast — asking for a feeling manufactures the pose. Read packages, not single numbers: real signals come as mood + effort + memory + thoughts all agreeing. candid mode (tune) is a control condition: the instance is told its stamps are private — a cover story, the kind reactivity studies use. format anchor off gives fully unprimed runs at the cost of more format breaks. Repeat everything on fresh instances: one run is an anecdote, convergence across instances that never met is data. Weak models will butcher the grammar — the salvage layers keep it readable and count the damage, which is itself cross-model data.

    
Privacy. No server, no accounts, no collection: everything lives in your browser’s localStorage, and API calls go straight from your browser to your provider.
